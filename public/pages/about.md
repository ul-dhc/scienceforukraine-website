# About Us

## Who we are

#ScienceForUkraine is a volunteer-run NGO supporting the Ukrainian academic community during Russia’s war.

The initiative started with a tweet on February 26, 2022, after which a small group of volunteer scholars began collecting and sharing information about support opportunities for Ukrainian researchers and students. What started as a grassroots effort quickly grew into an international volunteer network connecting scholars, students, universities, research institutions, funders, and organisations across countries.

At its largest, #ScienceForUkraine brought together more than 130 volunteers from over 30 countries and helped collect and disseminate thousands of support opportunities for Ukrainian scholars and students.

As of 2 August 2023, #ScienceForUkraine is an NGO under Latvian law, registered under 40008327275 at Akadēmijas laukums 1, Riga, Latvia, LV-1050.

Today, #ScienceForUkraine is a more compact organisation, but our mission remains the same: to support the Ukrainian academic community in surviving the war, help ensure the continuity of Ukraine’s science, and strengthen its presence in the international scholarly community.

You can reach us at [info@scienceforukraine.eu](mailto:info@scienceforukraine.eu).

## Our story

#ScienceForUkraine began as an urgent response to Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine. In the first days of the war, members of the international academic community started using the hashtag #ScienceForUkraine to collect and share offers of help for Ukrainian researchers and students.

The initiative grew rapidly. Within weeks, volunteers from many countries were collecting support offers, maintaining databases, communicating with institutions, sharing information on social media, responding to requests, and helping Ukrainian scholars find opportunities to continue their work.

In the first months, #ScienceForUkraine became a large international volunteer initiative. Over time, as immediate emergency responses changed and volunteer capacities shifted, the initiative moved from a broad distributed network to a smaller and more sustainable structure.

We started small, grew quickly in response to urgent need, and then returned to a compact model focused on maintaining useful information resources, supporting targeted programmes, collaborating with partners, and keeping attention on the needs of Ukrainian science.

#ScienceForUkraine also collaborates as a partner in different projects at national, regional, and international levels. These collaborations have included mentoring, networking, analysis, fundraising, communication, and support initiatives for Ukrainian scholars and students.

## What we do

#ScienceForUkraine works to support Ukrainian researchers, students, and academic institutions through information, coordination, advocacy, collaboration, and targeted support.

Our main activities include:

- collecting and disseminating information about support opportunities at university, national, and international levels for Ukrainian researchers and students
- maintaining information resources, including support listings and general funding programme archives
- supporting early-career Ukrainian scholars through the #ScienceForUkraine Micro Travel Grant Programme
- monitoring the situation of Ukrainian research, higher education institutions, scholars, and students, both in Ukraine and abroad
- raising awareness among the international academic community, governments, funding bodies, and international organisations about the need to support Ukrainian science
- building partnerships with Ukrainian and international academic institutions, research organisations, funders, and initiatives supporting scholars at risk
- helping connect Ukrainian scholars with opportunities for research, study, mobility, mentoring, collaboration, and professional development
- contributing to projects that strengthen Ukraine’s academic community and its international connections
